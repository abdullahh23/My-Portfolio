The Problem

Truck dispatchers waste hours every week on manual, repetitive work — entering load data by hand from Rate Confirmations, building invoices in spreadsheets, tracking payments across scattered documents, and analyzing revenue with no dashboard. It's slow, error-prone, and impossible to scale.

✅

The Solution

Load To Cash automates the entire workflow. Upload a Rate Confirmation — AI extracts all load data automatically. The platform generates professional invoices, tracks payments, monitors revenue, and gives dispatchers a real-time business dashboard with near-zero manual data entry.

Core Features

🤖

AI Document Processing

Google Gemini Flash automatically extracts load data from uploaded Rate Confirmations. No manual entry — the AI reads and fills everything.

📋

Invoice Generation

Professional invoices auto-generated with company branding, complete load details, dispatch fee calculations, and payment tracking.

📊

Business Dashboard

Real-time visibility into weekly loads, gross revenue, dispatch fees, and paid vs unpaid amounts. Business performance at a glance.

🚛

Carrier Management

Complete carrier history — invoice tracking, payment status, load history, and performance metrics per carrier relationship.

📁

Load Management

Organize all loads with route info, broker details, confirmation numbers, and pickup dates. No more spreadsheet chaos.

💰

Payment Tracking

Clear paid/unpaid status across all carriers and invoices. Know exactly what you're owed and what's been collected.

Challenges

  • Building a full SaaS platform while actively learning React, TypeScript, and Supabase simultaneously
  • Designing an AI integration that accurately extracts data from varied Rate Confirmation formats
  • Implementing secure multi-user authentication with proper data isolation from day one
  • Creating a UI that non-technical dispatchers can use without a learning curve
  • Making product decisions under uncertainty without a team or roadmap

Lessons Learned

  • Real users provide feedback that no amount of self-testing can replicate — ship early
  • Product decisions matter more than technical decisions at the early stage
  • AI works best with structured prompts and well-defined output formats
  • Security should be designed in from the start, not retrofitted later
  • Understanding the user's actual workflow before designing any feature is non-negotiable
